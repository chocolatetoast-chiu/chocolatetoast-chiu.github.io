Introduction

When we talk about the nuclear magnetic resonance, it’s better to get into the basic knowledge of investigating the phenomenon. Before that, let me introduce the concept of Spin. Then we will later introduce the concept of Magnetic Moment, finally Gyromagnetic Ratio.

Spin

Spin is actually the type of angular momentum possessed by atomic particles. So, spin can also be called spin angular momentum. Atomic particles include protons, neutrons, nuclei, and electrons.

property

But the funny point is that the particles themselves are not actually spinning. It’s just the natural property that is not produced by other things. So particles can be illustrated as the tiny ball that has the I angular momentum.

Interaction

Another characteristic of spin is that this property can be interacted with the magnetic field (B0), just like the angular momentum L to the gravity field.

Quantitative

The magnitude of spin is quantized, it can be only measured with full or half-integer (0, 1/2, 1, 3/2, 2, …). Electrons have only one spin value, which is S (1/2 always). And in MRI cases, 1H is commonly used for measurement. And it has the I value of 1/2.

Non-zero spin imaging

Only the nuclei with non-zero spin can absorb and emit electromagnetic radiation. So as to resonate with the applied magnetic field (B0).

And 1H only consists of just a single proton. So, the standard MRI signal usually comes from protons only due to the usage of 1H. But it should be avoided to use proton to explain the imaging of MRI when using nuclei other than hydrogen.

Magnetic Moment

It is a vector to measure the tendency of an object to interact with the external magnetic field. The object, in this case, can be referred to as molecules, atoms, nucleus, or some subatomic particles. And the magnetic moment of these objects can also be called Magnetic dipole moment (μ) due to the bar shape magnet visualization with the north and south poles.

μ will seek to align with the applied magnetic field(B0). The process will need to overcome the force called torque. Coming from μ X B0. After alignment, μ will be the lowest energy state with no torque; On the other hand, μ will be the highest energy state when pointing opposite to B0 direction. The formula of the energy of the magnetic moment is E = - μ·B0

Gyromagnetic Ratio

The gyromagnetic ratio (γ) is a constant used to represent the relationship between the spin (I) and the Magnetic dipole moment (μ). Here is the formula: μ = γI.

γ is different from various atomic species. Most of the nuclei have a positive value. But some nuclei and electrons are negative values. When γ > 0, it means that spin and magnetic dipole moment are in the same direction. γ < 0 means opposite directions, but still colinear.

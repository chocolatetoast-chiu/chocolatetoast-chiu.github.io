About Me

Hi! My name is Sheng-Chieh,

or you can call me Leon.

As a student of biomedical engineering, I have always been fascinated by advanced biomedical technologies.

For the specialty, my research projects focus on two major aspects:

  1. To develop novel image processing methods for molecular images, such as positron emission tomography (PET) and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), and to extract information that is relevant to neural degenerative diseases development and management.
  2. To develop software packages that automate image and data processing to achieve better efficiency in data analytics.

As for leadership and honor, in my junior year, I formed a new team for student competition with five other fellow students and served as the team leader. We implemented a project, “EmoSpace: A mobile game for emotion recognition training in children with autism spectrum disorder”. With this project, we competed in the 2019 Rehabilitation Engineering and Assistive Technology Society of North America (RESNA) Student Design Challenge and received the Third Place Award out of 79 international teams.

Nice to meet you guys!
